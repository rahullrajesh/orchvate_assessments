ADHD Self Assessment


    Instructions

    This self-assessment does not count as a diagnosis; it can help you to find out if you have any traits of ADHD. This will also give you a better understanding of how your brain works and for which tasks you may need additional support.


    The assessment is adapted from the Adult ADHD Self-Report Scale (ASRS-v1.1) Symptom Checklist that was developed in conjunction with the World Health Organization (WHO), and the Workgroup on Adult ADHD.


    The questions are consistent with DSM-IV criteria and address the manifestations of ADHD symptoms in adults. Six of the questions that were found to be the most predictive of symptoms consistent with ADHD form Part A. Part B contains the remaining twelve questions, that describe common ADHD symptoms.
